Traits

Every character type has a unique trait that makes it stand out. Survivors get to choose two traits, helping them specialise a little more.

SPECIAL Explanation

Every character is defined by these seven attributes, with a higher number representing greater ability. They can be closely compared to the D&D system of attributes.
When attempting skill checks, these attributes form part of the target number when rolling the dice.

Show derived statistics

Strength (STR) measures your character's physical prowess.

What it does: used to make melee attacks, increase melee damage, and changes how much you can carry
Buy ranks if: you want to be strong and solve problems with brute force

Carry weight: 150lbs + STR x 10
Penalties apply if overencumbered

A STR of 7 or higher boosts melee damage

Perception (PER) is how easily you notice danger, and how aware you are of the environment around you.

What it does: affects your initiative order, makes perception checks easier and improves your ability to use Energy Weapons
Buy ranks if: you want to be a perceptive person and solve problems by studying your surroundings

Initiative: PER + AGI

Endurance (END) is your natural toughness and influences how hard you are overall.

What it does: boosts your health points, improves your ability to shrug off physical dangers in the wasteland, and helps you wield heavy weapons
Buy ranks if: you want to be hardy and resilient, and solve problems by pushing through them

Max HP: END + LCK
You gain additional HP when levelling or when taking certain perks

Charisma (CHA) is how much people like you, and how well you can influence them in turn.

What it does: improves your effectiveness at convincing others, helps you barter with people and helps you find safety among others
Buy ranks if: you want to be diplomatic and persuasive, or you wish to solve problems by talking your way out of them


Intelligence (INT) measures your smarts and how well you can apply yourself to the wasteland.

What it does: improves your ability to assess obstacles and hazards, and helps you remember and know things by studying the world around you
Buy ranks if: you want to be cunning and clever, and find the most effective solution to your problems


Agility (AGL) measures how fit and agile you are, and helps make you quick and lean.

What it does: helps you to balance and control your body more precisely, and improves your ability to make ranged attacks with Small Guns
Buy ranks if: you want to be nimble, and want to solve problems with accuracy and skill

Initiative: PER + AGI

Defense is based on AGI - higher AGI makes you harder to hit
Defense is increased to 2 over 9 AGI

Luck (LCK) describes how much fortune smiles down on you, turning fickle forces in your favour.

What it does: helps you come out on top when you should be at the bottom, and could influence chances related to any event
Buy ranks if: you want to add story details, improve skill check results, or re-roll dice

Max HP: END + LCK
You gain additional HP when levelling or when taking certain perks

Skills

You are trained in several skills, which encompass the various activities and proficiencies that you've picked up surviving in the post-apocalyptic wasteland. Each skill ranked from 0 to 6, with each rank representing a differing degree of training.
You add your skill rank together with your SPECIAL attribute to get your target when making a skill check.

When initially allocating your skill points, you can only allocate up to a max of rank 3. Allocating a Tag skill sets the minimum rank of that skill to 2.

For each skill check, you'll roll a 1d20. To pass the check, you need to at least match 20 - (Skill + Attribute). For example, if your Lockpick rank is 3, and your Perception is 9, then your target is 20 - (3 + 9) = 8.

Additionally, if you are rolling a skill check using a Tag skill, then the critical success requirement decreases to 20 - Skill (so if Lockpick was tagged in the example above, the critical success boundary would be 20 - 3 = 17).

Perks

Every character has a personal edge, called perks. Perks are the specific advantages of your personal approach to challenges. They take the form of a mechanical bonus that apply to a specific circumstance.

Many perks have one or more requirements. These requirements must be met before you can select the perk for your character.

Each perk has a condition and a benefit. The condition describes the circumstances in which the perk can be used, and the benefit describes what you can do with the perk.

Action Boy/Girl

Ranks: 1

Requirements: None

When you spend AP to take an additional major action, you do not suffer the increased skill test difficulty during your second action.

Basher

Ranks: 1

Requirements: STR 6

When you make a melee attack by bashing with your gun, your attack gains the Vicious damage effect.

Black Widow/Lady Killer

Ranks: 1

Requirements: CHA 6

When you attempt a CHA based skill check to influence a character of the opposite gender, you may re-roll 1d20. In addition, your attacks inflict +1 Combat Dies worth of additional damage against the opposite gender.

Adrenaline Rush

Ranks: 1

Requirements: STR 7

When your health is below its maximum value, you count your STR score as 10 for all purposes when attempting a STR-base skill check or melee attack.

Better Criticals

Ranks: 1

Requirements: LCK 9

When you inflict one or more points of damage to an enemy, you may spend 1 Luck point to automatically inflict a critical hit.

Bloody Mess

Ranks: 1

Requirements: LCK 6

When you inflict a critical hit, roll 1 Combat Die; if you roll an Effect, you inflict one additional injury to a random location.

Awareness

Ranks: 1

Requirements: PER 7

When you take the Aim minor action at a target within Close range, you spot their weakness and can attack more efficiently. The next attack you make against that target gains Piercing 1 damage effect.

Big Leagues

Ranks: 1

Requirements: STR 8

When you make a melee attack with a two-handed weapon, the weapon gains the Vicious damage effect.

Can Do!

Ranks: 1

Requirements: LCK 5

When you are scavenging a location that contains food, you gain 1 additional random food item, without spending AP.
